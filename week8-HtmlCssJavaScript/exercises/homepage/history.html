History of Canyoning

Canyoning, also known as canyoneering, traces its roots to the mountainous regions of Europe, where adventurers began exploring and descending narrow gorges and canyons in the early 20th century. The sport evolved from the need for mountaineers to navigate challenging terrain and has since transformed into a thrilling outdoor activity that combines various skills, including climbing, rappelling, and swimming.

The development of canyoning gained momentum in the 1960s and 1970s, especially in the mountainous regions of the French and Spanish Pyrenees. As equipment and techniques improved, canyoning became more accessible to a broader audience, attracting outdoor enthusiasts seeking an adrenaline-fueled experience amid nature's stunning landscapes.

Today, canyoning has become a global phenomenon, with enthusiasts exploring canyons on every continent. It has evolved into a diverse and exciting activity, ranging from beginner-friendly trips to advanced, technical descents. The history of canyoning is a testament to humanity's enduring fascination with pushing boundaries and discovering the hidden wonders of our natural surroundings.
